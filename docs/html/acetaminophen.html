Acetaminophen

<>"Tylenol" redirects here. For the brand, see TYLENOL (brand).
Acetaminophen (also referred to as tylenol) is an oral medication over-the-counter analgesic most often used to relieve headache, fever, and other minor pain. Acetaminophen comes in many forms, including as capsule (medicine), caplet, and tablet (medicine) of various sizes, including 200 miligram, 325 mg, and most often 500 mg. Acetaminophen may also be combined with ibuprofen, an Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug. Acetaminophen's chemical formula is Carbons-b8Hydrogens-b9NitrogenOxygens-b2. Acetaminophen is a colorless, odorless solid at room temperature and can cause skin irritation and eye irritation. Consuming the standalone chemical at large doses is acute toxicity and can be harmful to aquatic life.
