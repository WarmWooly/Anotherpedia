Boiler (Boiled)

<>The boiler is a block (Minecraft) in the Minecraft mod (Minecraft) Boiled (Minecraft mod) used to boil item (Minecraft). The boiler can be crafting (Minecraft) with 7 copper ingot (Minecraft), 1 furnace (Minecraft), and 1 water bucket (Minecraft) (place the bucket in the center, the furnace below, and surround with copper). The boiler can be fuelled with any fuel (Minecraft) used for furnace (Minecraft)/blast furnace (Minecraft)/smoker (Minecraft), adding 1 unit of heat for each tick (Minecraft) that it is fuelled (20 heat/second). For example, a coal (Minecraft) will give 1,600 heat over 80 seconds. The maximum heat of a normal boiler is 6,000. More heat speeds up the process of boiling, with 2,000 heat matching the speed of a furnace, and 4,000 being twice that. The boiler needs at least 100 heat to start boiling. For every tick the boiler is unfuelled, it will lose 1 unit of heat.

When removing the end result from a boiler, it will return the total experience (Minecraft) rounded to the nearest whole number. The following are the recipes that can be performed by a boiler (time based on 2,000 heat):<> Entities standing atop an active boiler with a heat of at least 1,000 will take damage (Minecraft) every 0.5 seconds. The damage is proportional to the heat, with every 1,000 heat dealing a half a heart. Fire Resistance (Minecraft) negates boiling damage. The boiler can interact with other blocks around it to expand its capabilities. If a block of copper (Minecraft) is placed above the boiler, it will double its heat capacity to 12,000. Every "hot" block placed adjacent to a boiler will passively heat the boiler. The effects of multiple heating blocks do not stack (only 1 heat/tick), but they increase the cap of how much heat can be passively added (1 block = 1,000 heat, 2 blocks = 2,000 heat, etc). Fuel can still be used while the boiler is passively heated.
