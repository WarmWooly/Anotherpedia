Caramel

{{tFor other terms related to caramel, see caramel (disambiguation).}}&sp{{bCaramel}} is a confectionery produce by heating sugar, which is used as a topping for ice cream, candy filling in candy, and flavoring in various dessert. The process to make caramel is known as caramelization, where sugar is slowly heated to 340 °fahrenheit, which breaks down the sugar. Caramel can be eating in both a liquid and solid form. When a liquid, caramel is highly viscous and sticky.
