Caramel

For other terms related to caramel, see caramel (disambiguation).&spCaramel is a confectionery produce by heating sugar, which is used as a topping for ice cream, filling in candy, and flavoring in various desserts. The process to make caramel is known as caramelization, where sugar is slowly heated to 340 °F, which breaks down the sugar. Caramel can be eaten in both a liquid and solid form. When a liquid, caramel is highly viscous and sticky.
