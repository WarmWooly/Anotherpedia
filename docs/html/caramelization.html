Caramelization

Caramelization is the process of liquifying sugar by slowly heating it, food browning it. The main purpose is to sweetening food or to make caramel. It is often done with pure sugar or with vegetable such as onion. The exact chemical reaction which takes place are still being study as it is a complex interaction which can change based on the sugar or food item being caramelized. chemical such as diacetyl are released, providing the caramel flavor, and the polymer caramelan, caramelan, and caramelin, providing the caramel (color).
