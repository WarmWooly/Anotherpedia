Caramelization

<><>{{bCaramelization}} is the [[process]] of [[liquifying]] [[sugar]] by slowly [[heating]] it, [[browning|food browning]] it. The main purpose is to [[sweeten|sweetening]] [[food]] or to make [[caramel]]. It is often done with pure sugar or with [[vegetables|vegetable]] such as [[onions|onion]]. The exact [[chemical reactions|chemical reaction]] which takes place are still being [[studied|study]] as it is a [[complex]] interaction which can change based on the sugar or food item being caramelized. [[Chemicals|chemical]] such as [[diacetyl]] are released, providing the caramel [[flavor]], and the [[polymers|polymer]] [[caramelans|caramelan]], [[caramelans|caramelan]], and [[caramelins|caramelin]], providing the [[color|caramel (color)]].
