Chain Launcher (Not Lazy Chainmail)

<>The chain launcher is an item (Minecraft) in the Minecraft mod (Minecraft) Not Lazy Chainmail used for player (Minecraft) traversal though being launched. The chain launcher can be crafting (Minecraft) with 1 lever (Minecraft), 1 piston (Minecraft), 1 chain (Minecraft), 4 block of iron chains (Not Lazy Chainmail), and 1 spool block (Not Lazy Chainmail) (place the piston in the center, the chain to the right, the spool block to the left, the lever in the bottom left corner, and fill the top, bottom, and right corners with blocks of iron chains). The chain launcher can be used to launch in the direction the player is facing. Using the launcher puts it on cooldown (Minecraft). The chain launcher has multiple interactions and enchantment (Minecraft) that change how the chain launcher can be used. By holding a vinegar bottle (Not Lazy Chainmail) in the offhand (Minecraft), the launch power is increased by 214.3%, "boosting" the launch. An empty glass bottle (Minecraft) will be placed in the inventory (or dropped if full) on use. Boosting while airborne (with the Ascension (Not Lazy Chainmail) enchantment) will award the player the advancement (Minecraft) Flight in All but Name (Not Lazy Chainmail). By holding a ropes (Not Lazy Chainmail) in the offhand, the rope will be launched from the launcher instead of boosting the player. If the rope hits a block (Minecraft), it will act as if it has been placed and create a climbable rope. If the rope hits a mob (Minecraft), it will deal significant damage and drop as an item on the ground. The following ropes are ordered from weakest to strongest: copper rope (Not Lazy Chainmail), iron rope (Not Lazy Chainmail), gold rope (Not Lazy Chainmail), rose gold rope (Not Lazy Chainmail), netherite rope (Not Lazy Chainmail), and iron composite rope (Not Lazy Chainmail). Hitting a mob with an iron composite rope will award the advancement Evildoers, Beware! (Not Lazy Chainmail) By punch (Minecraft) a mob (Minecraft) with a chain launcher, they will be flung in the direction the attacker is facing. This action can also be boosted with a vinegar bottle, and will award the player the advancement We're Blasting Off Again! (Not Lazy Chainmail) As launching other mobs is quite powerful, to compensate, the cooldown is x5 as long as performing the self-propelled equivalents. Ropes will not be launched when punching mobs. By holding a bucket with a mob (bucket of cod (Minecraft), bucket of salmon (Minecraft), bucket of tropical fish (Minecraft), bucket of pufferfish (Minecraft), bucket of axolotl (Minecraft), or bucket of tadpole (Minecraft)) in the offhand, the mob in the bucket will be flung out. The bucket in the offhand will become a water bucket (Minecraft). By holding a fire charge (Minecraft) in the offhand, it will release a small fireball (Minecraft) (as if shot by a dispenser (Minecraft)). By holding a splash potion (Minecraft) or lingering potion (Minecraft) in the offhand, it will shoot out the potion. Launching potions does not affect the potion's duration or quality. By holding an end crystal (Minecraft) in the offhand, a projectile will be launched out that will place an end crystal when it lands. It will place an end crystal regardless of landing on the ground or hitting a mob. Because of how powerful the launching end crystals can be, it has a x2.5 longer cooldown compared to other projectiles. By holding a loose stone (Not Lazy Chainmail), a loose deepened stone (Not Lazy Chainmail), or a mudball (Not Lazy Chainmail), a projectile will be launched out of the corresponding material. Loose stones deal damage, break glass (Minecraft), and drop on the ground. Loose deepened stones deal more damage than loose stones. If a player increases their Rockiness with rocky leather padding (Not Lazy Chainmail), loose stones have increased damage. Mudballs deal no damage and do not drop on the ground, but they do give hit mobs Slowness (Minecraft) and Blindness (Minecraft) for 5 seconds. They only have half the cooldown compared to other projectiles. cooldown (Minecraft) is dependent on enchantments and the action performed.
