Chainmail armor (Not Lazy Chainmail)

<>Chainmail armor is a category of armor (Minecraft) in the Minecraft Minecraft modding Not Lazy Chainmail. Chainmail armor is comprised of chain links (Not Lazy Chainmail) with a chainmail project (Not Lazy Chainmail). Unlike normal armors, chainmail armor can have leather paddings (Not Lazy Chainmail) applied to them to increase their stats and apply unique buffs. There are six types of chainmail armor: chainmail armor (Minecraft), gold chainmail armor (Not Lazy Chainmail), copper chainmail armor (Not Lazy Chainmail), netherite chainmail armor (Not Lazy Chainmail), rose gold chainmail armor (Not Lazy Chainmail), and iron composite chainmail armor (Not Lazy Chainmail). Helmets require 320 chain links, chestplates require 512, leggings require 448, and boots require 256. The chainmail helemt (Minecraft), chainmail chestplate (Minecraft), gold chainmail helmet (Not Lazy Chainmail), and gold chainmail chestplate (Not Lazy Chainmail) each have a 7.69% chance to appear in a suspicious sand (Minecraft) within a chainmail ruins (Not Lazy Chainmail). Chainmail armor can be equipped to the four armor slots: head, chest, legs, and feet. Only one of each may be applied at a time. Chainmail armor can be manually placed in the slot or automatically swapped by using the armor in the main hand (Minecraft)/offhand (Minecraft). While the player (Minecraft) is wearing at least one gold or rose gold chainmail armor piece, piglin (Minecraft) will be neutral instead of hostile. This effect does not apply to piglin brute (Minecraft). In a leathersmithing table (Not Lazy Chainmail), up to 3 leather paddings (Not Lazy Chainmail) can be applied to chainmail armor. Leather paddings will change the stats on the piece, increasing various stats dependent on the applied padding. Leather paddings are lost when chainmail armor breaks or is crusher (Not Lazy Chainmail) Chainmail armor can be enchanting (Minecraft) with Unbreaking (Minecraft), Mending (Minecraft), Curse of Binding (Minecraft), Curse of Vanishing (Minecraft), Protection (Minecraft), Blast Protection (Minecraft), Fire Protection (Minecraft), Projectile Protection (Minecraft), Thorns (Minecraft), Aqua Affinity (Minecraft), Respiration (Minecraft), Swift Sneak (Minecraft), Feather Falling (Minecraft), Frost Walker (Minecraft), Depth Strider (Minecraft), and Soul Speed (Minecraft) using an enchanted book (Minecraft) on an anvil (Minecraft). Unbreaking, Protection, Blast Protection, Fire Protection, Projectile Protection, Thorns, Aqua Affinity, Respiration, Feather Falling, Frost Walker, and Depth Strider may also be obtained in an enchanting table (Minecraft). Chainmail armor can be repaired through combining (Minecraft) by crafting (Minecraft), using a grindstone (Minecraft), or using an anvil (Minecraft). Combining two pieces of the same chainmail armor will add the two durability values together with an additional 3 durability. Combining will remove all enchantments, with some experience (Minecraft) returned when combining in a grindstone. Combining in an anvil will keep and combine the enchantments on the two chainmail pieces, costing experience. In an anvil (Minecraft), chainmail armor can be repaired using its corresponding ingot (iron ingot (Minecraft), gold ingot (Minecraft), copper ingot (Minecraft), netherite ingot (Minecraft), rose gold ingot (Not Lazy Chainmail), or iron composite ingot (Not Lazy Chainmail)), costing experience (Minecraft) and restoring 25% of the peice's durability, rounded down. The runes (Not Lazy Chainmail) Chain Repair Rune (Not Lazy Chainmail) can be applied to chainmail armor, costing 3 experience (Minecraft) with the max Chain Repair (Not Lazy Chainmail) level at IV. In a smithing table (Minecraft), armor trim (Minecraft) can be applied to chainmail armor with a material (amethyst shard (Minecraft), chromatic mass (Not Lazy Chainmail), copper ingot (Minecraft), diamond (Minecraft), emerald (Minecraft), gold ingot (Minecraft), iron ingot (Minecraft), iron composite ingot (Not Lazy Chainmail), lapis lazuli (Minecraft), nether quartz (Minecraft), netherite ingot (Minecraft), redstone dust (Minecraft), rose gold ingot (Not Lazy Chainmail), and telemagnetic alloy (Not Lazy Chainmail)) to add a trimming (Minecraft) to the armor. Trims are purely cosmetic, with their coloration based on the material used. Chainmail armor can be crusher (Not Lazy Chainmail) into an unfinished projects (Not Lazy Chainmail) of its corresponding material, which can be snipping (Not Lazy Chainmail) to recover all the chain links (Not Lazy Chainmail) used to make it. Iron chainmail armor can be smelting (Minecraft) in a furnace (Minecraft) or a blast furnace (Minecraft) to produce an iron nugget (Minecraft), taking 10 and 5 seconds respectively.
