Chapter 1 (Sans Obby)

<>
<>{{bChapter 1: Sans Obby}} is the first chapter of the Roblox game Sans Obby. <>, it is the only finished chapter. It is planned to follow Chapter 0 (Sans Obby) and Chapter 2 (Sans Obby). After the ending of Sans Obby Chapter 0: Will I Hear The Clock Stop At The End, the player manages to fall deep underground to the hidden entrance of Reactor 174 (Sans Obby). This is where the player meets Sans and is given the task of helping him get his ketchup back after it was recently stolen by Bara O. Bama. Once the player crosses the first obstacles of the game and makes it to the Target Corporation, they will meet Sans outside of it, where he will hint at the next puzzle the player must find a solution for and foreshadow the upcoming code door. The player can walk through the glass doors of the Super Target where the checkpoints are. Inside the Super Target, the player will encounter Sans at the Guest Services area, Goku at the Pizza Hut, Grug at the deli area, Lois Griffin at the bakery area, and the Flesh Prison at the CVS Pharmacy area. Sans's dialogue will reveal to the player that there is someone who is supposed to be working at the Guest Services area, but is not currently there. Talking to Goku will inform the player that the back exit of the Super Target is, in fact, behind an intangible wall, and that the path they're going down is a very dangerous one, but that Goku believes in their strength to handle it. If the player goes to talk to Grug, they will be met with a caveman screaming and aggressive grunting. However, Grug's dialogue will prove useful as it will reveal the general direction the player should be searching in, around the CVS Pharmacy area. Lois will prove to be the most direct and forward out of the others in the Super Target, as she will more directly indicate to the player what is to come, a code door, and that the only person who knows the code is the person in charge of Customer Services. She will warn the player that this person is a member of the Yakuza (Criminal Organization), but not be too scared of him, and that he is currently located outside of the building somewhere. The last NPC in the Super Target is the Flesh Prison, who provides nothing at all and simply spinning object meme. Finally, located outside of the Super Target in the back right, is Goro Majima, the man in charge of Customer Services. Goro Majima is expecting Kiryu to show up soon, so he quickly shoos the player away by directly telling the player the code for the code door and telling them to leave immediately. After getting the code, the player will simply have to press the correct keys on the doors keypad and click the keypad's enter key to continue on. The player will have to overcome 5 more obstacles before encountering Sans again. Sans will encourage the player by saying they've done a good job and that all they need to do now is make it to the reactor exhaust zone in front of them. The player will very soon encounter Sans again after the next two obstacles. He will once again encourage the player before saying the player is close to a door leading to somewhere he doesn't know where it goes, before laughing a little. After that brief laugh, Sans will get a little serious in the form of commenting on the fact that whatever is behind that door messes with spacetime, only to quickly cut himself off and tell the player to ignore those comments. This part of the game also marks an important choice that will be brutal for gameplay, given the obstacle. Depending on whether the player chooses the left or right, they will encounter different dialogues with the Mysterious Stranger Over The Intercoms. The left path will warn the player that the rods in front of them are so radioactivity from still ongoing reactions that they will kill them if they go on the wrong side of them. The right path will be slightly more insulting towards the player, but still warn them of the danger in front of them. When the player makes it to the other side of the rods, they will be met with the final puzzle of the game. It's one last code door and a dialogue with the Mysterious Stranger Over The Intercoms. He will compliment the player on making it this far. If the player chooses to ask for help from this man, he will say that he can. When the player figures out the code to the door, they will be given access to the final dialogue of the game and be presented with where they'll have to go next. The last dialogue will have the Mysterious Stranger Over The Intercoms explain to you that before you is a Garganta (Sans Obby). It was put there by him, and he calls it a shortcut to the location of the man he presumes you're currently after, Bara O. Bama. It is also in this room that the player will be rewarded a Badges (Roblox) for completing the first chapter. Jumping into the Garganta will close out the game and will be what sends the player to Sans Obby Chapter 2: Sans Incremental. <> The first obstacle is a platforming one where the player must jump on the correct square to progress. Some squares have no collision, making the player fall through them into the inky black void that will kill them. Giving each square a number starting from left to right in a given row of them, then the solution would be 2, 1, 2, 2, 3, 2. The second obstacle is a pathway-based one where the player must pick the correct path to progress. Some paths, like the squares in the first obstacle, have no collision. This can be made easier by using the camera to check for collision by moving your camera into the path you want to test. If it goes right through it, then it doesn't have collision. This obstacle also showcases the first of the game's many unfair gameplay aspects. After the 2nd path, the gray section for the start of the 3rd has no collision. Giving each of the tan pathways a number starting from left to right, the solution would be 1, 3, or 7, then 5, then jump to 4, 2, or 6. It should be noted that after the 2nd path, the gray section for the start of the 4th does have collision. While the jump to Obstacle 3 can be done from 6, it is recommended to do it from 2. The third obstacle, and the last of the opening section of the game, is a simple Kill brick (Roblox) path. Just walk through the pathway without touching the kill bricks, and you will be fine. By walking under the sign for the Super Target, you have officially entered the Super Target portion of the game, which poses no risk of death in this current version of the game. <>First, talk to Lois at the Bakery. She will tell you where to find the man who knows the code for the code door. Head outside of the Super Target and find Goro Majima. Talking to him will give you the code for the door. Simply convert the date he tells you into a set of numbers using Day/Month/Year format. Head back inside the building and talk to Grug. He is located right by Lois. Pay attention to Grug's dialogue for the general direction of the building to search in. Locate the wall with the freezer of a different color. When facing that freezer and looking at it, head to the far right of that wall. Then, once you reach the end, turn left, and that is the face you must go through. When you go through this wall, you may think you are stuck; however, you are not. There will be another intangible wall you will need to go through before finally exiting to the other side of the building. This wall is of a very noticeable different color. Go through it and head to the code door. This code door will be located on the right. Going down the left will just reach a dead end. Simply enter the correct code into the door, and it will open to allow you to progress to the next stage of the game. <> Obstacle 4 is a tight stair jumping gauntlet. Touching the bright red kill bricks will kill you. This obstacle features an unfair mechanic towards the end. On the last staircase, the entire down section is intangible. The player will fall through those stairs to their death. However, the bright red bricks on those stairs will not kill the player if they touch them, allowing the player to clear this obstacle. To complete the final staircase, at the last step in the up section, jump over the two kill bricks at the top and land on the bright red neon brick in the down section. Simply jump on those bricks at the end, and you will clear this obstacle. The fifth obstacle is a kill brick checkerboard. What kills you in this obstacle alternates with every row cleared. The first one has the floor kill you, the second has the kill bricks kill you, and this pattern alternates. Eventually, you will reach a gap. When you do, jump onto the kill bricks. The first two rows here are intended to troll the player by both having the floor kill you after establishing a pattern in your brain. However, the 3rd one has the kill bricks kill you, and the pattern starts alternating again. Simply make it through without dying, and you will clear this obstacle. This obstacle tasks the player to make it through to the end of it under the pressure of the ground beneath their feet disappearing. This one is very simple. All that needs to be done is to avoid the kill bricks by booking it for the other side. Do not slow down for even a moment, or the ground will disappear from under you, causing you to fall to your death. This one is also quite simple. Just balance on the thin platforms and jump over the kill bricks. It shouldn't be that hard. The reason for the lack of difficulty is that this obstacle was originally the very first one, but as development of the overhaul from the original Sans Obby continued, this was later moved to be right here. Obstacle 8 is a simple mix of jumping over and not running into kill bricks. The part where you have to jump over the kill bricks after avoiding the long bar kill bricks is in fact Loss (meme). After jumping over Loss, it's just a little bit more of simple jumping to make it to the 9th Obstacle. The 9th Obstacle tasks the player with jumping over a large gap on balls. Simply find the correct order of balls to jump on to make it to the other end. The tenth obstacle has the player jump across parts of a checkerboard. Only some of these squares have collision. Jumping on one without collision will make the player touch a kill brick underneath the checkerboard, which immediately kills the player. To clear this obstacle, start on the 10th square of the first row. Then jump over the black squares to the white squares in front of you up until the 2nd-to-last row. From that white square you should now be on, jump to the black square by the right-most wall. From there, simply jump over the gap, and you have completed this obstacle. More solutions may be possible, so feel free to deviate and see what works. The 11th obstacle is the second-to-last obstacle of the game. There is a left and right variant, separated by a green neon part that will kill you on contact. Between you and the 12th obstacle is a series of diamond-shaped kill bricks. Simply jump to the gaps between them to clear this obstacle. This is the final obstacle of the game. This obstacle has a left and right variant. Obstacle 12 has the player walk through the gaps between gray parts. These gaps have invisible parts in them. If the player picks the wrong side when travelling through, the player will be immediately killed. Touching the gray parts will also kill the player. This obstacle features an unfair gameplay mechanic in the form of the right variant being impossible to complete, as both sides at the end kill the player. This obstacle can only be completed by choosing the left variant. The solution for the left variant is this: right, right, right, right, left, left, left, right, left, left, left, left. In the current version of the game, the puzzle has no hints. Some will eventually be added. The code is the ID for the game. Simply enter the correct code and walk through the door to end the game and be awarded a badge for doing so.
