Clad coin

See also: Plated coin
A clad coin is a coin with a core made out of one metal and cladding (metalworking) with another. This is primarily done to reduce the cost of coining (mint) coins by utilizing a cheaper coinage metals while using a higher-value metal as the external layer. The core is sandwiching between two layers of another metal before being clad and stamped.
