Coal (Minecraft)

<resistance|Noinfo>>Coal is an item in Minecraft that is often used in smelting. When coal ore is mined with a pickaxe, it will drop 1 coal. When mined with Fortune, it has a chance to drop an extra piece of coal with each level, with a maximum of 4 coal using Fortune III. Silk Touch will prevent the ore from dropping coal. Coal can be crafted with 1 block of coal, which will produce 9 coal. Coal ore can be smelted in a furnace or blast furnace to produce 1 coal, alongside experience. The chances to find coal in chests are the following (same for Bedrock and Java unless specified):
In a dungeon, a chest has a 26.6% chance of containing 1--4 coal.
In a mineshaft, a chest has a 27.3% chance of containing 3--8 coal.
In an ancient city, a chest has a 46.5% chance of containing 6--15 coal.
In an igloo, a chest has a 70.4% chance of containing 1--4 coal.
In a shipwreck, a supply chest has a 37.3% chance of containing 2--8 coal.
In ocean ruins, a small ruins chest has an 84.2% chance of containing 1--4 coal and a large ruins chest has an 81% chance of containing 1--4 coal.
In a woodland mansion, a chest has a 26.6% chance of containing 1--4 coal.
In a stronghold, a storeroom chest has a 28.5%s-p(Bedrock only) chance or a 34.4%s-p(Java only) chance of containing 3-8 coal.
In a village:
&tabA chest in a Toolsmith's house has a 9.9% chance of containing 1--3 coal.
&tabA chest in a Butcher's house has a 27.9% chance of containing 1--3 coal.
&tabA chest in a Fisherman's house has a 43%s-p(Java only) chance of containing 1--3 coal.
&tabA chest in a snowy house has a 41.2% chance of containing 1--4 coal. The chances to find coal in suspicious blocks are the following (same for Bedrock and Java unless specified):
In ocean ruins, a suspicious block has a 13.3% chance of containing 1 coal.
In trail ruins, a suspicious block has a 2.2%s-p(Java only) chance of containing 1 coal. Wither skeletons have a 33.3% chance of dropping 1 coal on death. When slain with Looting, it has a chance to drop 1 more coal per level. The chance for it to drop coal is 1 - 2 / (Looting + 3), which equals out to 1/3, 1/2, 3/5, and 2/3 for no Looting, I, II, and III respectively. The chance to get more coal with Looting is equally spread (ex. Looting II --> 40% 0 coal, 20% 1 coal, 20% 2 coal, 20% 3 coal). Coal is used in the following crafting recipes:
Block of Coal - 9 coal (filling all available slots)
Campfire - 1 coal, 3 sticks, and 3 logs, stripped logs, wood, or stripped wood (the coal goes in the center, with the wood filling the bottom row, and the sticks surrounding the other three sides of the coal)
Fire Charge x3 - 1 coal, 1 blaze powder, and 1 gunpowder (shapeless)
Soul Torch x4 - 1 coal, 1 stick, and 1 soul sand or soul soil (the soul sand/soil goes on the bottom, with a stick above it, then the coal)
Torch x4 - 1 coal and 1 stick (the stick goes on the bottom, with a coal above it) In a furnace, blast furnace, or smoker, 1 coal can fuel for 80 seconds (8 items), with a maximum of 1 stack (64 coal, 5120 seconds, 512 items).
In a minecart with furnace, 1 coal can fuel for 3 minutes (720 meters, 3600 ticks), with a maximum of just over 9 coal (~27.3 minutes, ~6553.4 meters, 32767 ticks). For Novice-level fishermen, there is a 50% chance to trade 10 coal for 1 emerald.
For Novice-level armorers, there is a 100%s-p(Bedrock only) chance or a 40%s-p(Java only) to trade 15 coal for 1 emerald.
For Novice-level toolsmiths, there is a 100%s-p(Bedrock only) chance or a 40%s-p(Java only) to trade 15 coal for 1 emerald.
For Novice-level weaponsmiths, there is a 100%s-p(Bedrock only) chance or a 66.6%s-p(Java only) to trade 15 coal for 1 emerald.
For Apprentice-level butchers, there is a 100%s-p(Bedrock only) chance or a 66.6%s-p(Java only) to trade 15 coal for 1 emerald.
