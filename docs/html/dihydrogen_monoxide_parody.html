Dihydrogen monoxide parody

<><>{{bDihydrogen monoxide parody}} is the [[phenomenon]] of calling [[water]] by an unfamiliar name, and then listing [[properties|Chemical property]] of water to sound [[dangerous|Danger]], despite being [[normal]] when worded differently. Some examples are that dihydrogen monoxide can cause [[suffocation]] ([[drowning]]), [[erosion]] of [[landscape|Land]], and [[accelerated|Acceleration]] [[metal]] [[corrosion]] ([[rusting]]), while being used in [[nuclear power plants|Nuclear Power Plant]] and an [[additive]] in many [[food]] products.
