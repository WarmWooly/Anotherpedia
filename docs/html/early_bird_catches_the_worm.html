Early bird catches the worm

<><>{{bEarly bird catches the worm}}, or sometimes {{bearly bird gets the worm}}, is an [[idiom]] which states that those who are prepared to [[complete]] a [[task]] are more likely get a [[reward]]. This is because a [[bird]] is more likely to catch a [[worm]] if it looks for it before other birds. The idiom's first recorded use was in 1605 in a [[book]] by [[William Camden]]. The idiom is often shortened to {{bearly bird}}, being [[synonymous|synonym]] to an early riser.
