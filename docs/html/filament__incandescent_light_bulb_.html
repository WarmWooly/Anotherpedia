Filament (incandescent light bulb)

<><>A {{b[[filament]]}} in an [[incandescent light bulb]] is a thin [[conducting|conduction (electricity)]] [[coiled|coil]] [[wire]] that connect to the contact wires, which [[glows|incandescent]] due to [[heat]] generated by the [[resistance|resistance (electricity)]] to the [[electricity]] flowing through the filament. [[Tungsten]] is the most used material for filaments due to tungsten's ability to withstand heat for long periods of time while conducting electricity.
