Flint

For other uses of the term flint, see flint (disambiguation).
Flint is a type of mineral quartz as a type of chert found inside of chalk or marly limestone. The most notable use of flint is the ability to make tool and weapon out of it, alongside starting fire.

Flint was invaluable in the Stone Age, being sought out for its versatility. This is mainly due to the ability to easily chip the edges of flint into sharp formations. In North America, Ohio was one of the key sources of flint during the Stone Age. Due to this, Ohio flint is the List of U.S. state minerals, rocks, stone, and gemstones of Ohio. knife, axe, spear, and arrowhead were some of the many tools and weapons made with flint as the main component.

Flint can also be used to ignition flammable materials with the aid of a metal, such as steel and pyrite. This was used for flintlock, where the flint would strike a steel, igniting gunpowder used to fire a bullet.
