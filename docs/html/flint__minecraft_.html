Flint (Minecraft)

<resistance|Noinfo>>Flint is an item in Minecraft that is often used in crafting tools and weapons. Flint can be obtained by mining gravel, where there is a 10% chance that flint will drop instead of gravel on destruction. When mined with Fortune, it increases to 16%, 25%, and 100% with I, II, and III respectively. Silk Touch and falling on non-solid blocks will never cause gravel to drop flint. In both Bedrock and Java, the chances to find flint in chests are the following:
A chest by a ruined portal has a 46.4% chance to contain 1-4 flint.
In a village, a chest in a Fletcher's house will have a 55.8% chance of containing 1-3 flint. When trading, there is a 50%s-p(Bedrock only) chance or a 66.67%s-p(Java only) chance for a Novice-level fletcher to trade 10 flint for 10 gravel and 1 emerald. Flint is used in the following crafting recipes:
Arrow x4 - 1 flint, 1 stick, and 1 feather (with each item listed stacked on top of each other, from top to bottom)
Fletching Table - 2 flint and 4 planks (make a 2x2 square of planks on the bottom, then place the flint above it)
Flint and Steel - 1 flint and 1 iron ingot (shapeless) For Apprentice-level fletchers, there is always a trade of 26 flint for an emerald.
For Apprentice-level leatherworkers, there is a 100%s-p(Bedrock only) chance or a 66.67%s-p(Java only) chance to trade 26 flint for an emerald.
For Journeyman-level toolsmith, there is a 100%s-p(Bedrock only) chance or a 40%s-p(Java only) chance to trade 30 flint for an emerald.
For Journeyman-level weaponsmith, there is always a trade of 24 flint for an emerald.
