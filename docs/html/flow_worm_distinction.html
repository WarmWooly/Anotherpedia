Flow worm distinction

Flow worm distinction is the breakdown of the coloration and poses that flow worms in Bits & Bops take on. For this page, "primary" will be the main body color, "secondary" will be the colors of the limbs and facial patch, "facial" will be the eyes and mouth outline, "mouth" will be the inside of the mouth, and "outline" will be the body's outline. The first six and the last flow worm will be called "NPC 1--7" respectively, and the seventh player-controlled one will be called "Player". The only balled-up flow worm to be discussed will be the slow one (called "Slow") as it has unique coloration.

In general, the NPC flow worms during the day share the same secondary, facial, mouth, and outline colors. All NPC flow worms during the night also have the same facial and mouth colors. Day flow worms have an outline darker than their primary color, but the outline is lighter at night. The night palette is very saturated (based on primary and secondary colors), with the least saturated worms being NPC 5 and 7 during the Sky Mixtape at 73% in HSL, followed up by NPC 4 in Flow Worms with 76%. In the minigame Flow Worms, there are eight flow worms lined up along the bottom. During the night segment, an additional crowd of 30 flow worms (not all visible in-game; colors will be based on the sprite file, with each member named left to right "Crowd 1--10") is present in the background. Only the brightest row of the crowd will be listed in the table/palette, as the second and third rows are the same poses and colors darkened by ~15% and ~30% respectively. Poses between the day and night segments remain unchanged. The default pose has raised antennae, moving top legs, open eyes, and a closed mouth. The same day sprites are used in Final Mixtape.<> Flow worms reappear the mixtape Sky Mixtape, with all NPCs sporting a halo and a pair of small, 2-segmented wings. All flow worms also share the same poses as in Flow Worms, with the exception that the slow flow worm at night does not uncurl. The halos during the night do not have a secondary glint color.
