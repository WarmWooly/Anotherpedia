Fluoroantimonic acid

Fluoroantimonic acid is a mixture between hydrogen fluoride and antimony pentafluoride. Fluroantimonic acid comes in the form of a colorless liquid. Fluoroantimonic acid's chemical formula is SbHF6. It is considered corrosive due to how acidic and a health hazard due to being lethal on digestion or inhalation, severe skin burns, and a potential carcinogen.

Fluoroantimonic acid is considered a superacid due to being trillions of times more acidic than 100% sulfuric acid, with a H0 of -28. The reaction between hydrogen fluoride and antimony pentafluoride is the following:SbF5 + 2HF &equ SbF6− + H2F+The true reaction is more complex, but this is a good approximation of the reaction. The reaction is exothermic, where fluorine is stripped from a hydrogen fluoride alongside the hydrogen's electron as they combine with the antimony pentafluoride. This makes the hexafluoroantimonate ion (SbF6-). The single hydrogen atom combines with another hydrogen fluoride to make the fluoronium (H2F+).
