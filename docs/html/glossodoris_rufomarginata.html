Glossodoris rufomarginata

{{bGlossodoris rufomarginata}} is a species of sea slug by many names, being a nudibranch (a gastropod mollusk) in the family of Chromodorididae. Growing up to 2 inches long, a Glossodoris rufomarginata is a solid white covered in orangish-brown dots, with a thick white wavy brim tipped with the same brown speckles. The edge of its foot is a solid white. Its rhinophores (eyes) and branchial plume (back structure) can be retracted, sharing similar coloration as the rest of its body.&pIt can be found in many parts of the Indo-Pacific, with the main populations located near Hawaii and Sri Lanka. It prefers to stay hidden in reefs, rocks, or in shade. They are mainly active during the day, congregating near sea sponges to feed.
