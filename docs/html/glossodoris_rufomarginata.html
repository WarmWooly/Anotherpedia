Glossodoris rufomarginata

{{bGlossodoris rufomarginata}} is a species of sea slug by many names, being a nudibranch (a gastropod Mollusca) in the family (biology) of Chromodorididae. Growing up to 2 inch long, a Glossodoris rufomarginata is a solid white covered in orangish-brown dots, with a thick white wavy brim tipped with the same brown speckles. The edge of its foot is a solid white. Its rhinophore (eyes) and branchial plume (back structure) can be retraction, sharing similar coloration as the rest of its body.

It can be found in many parts of the Indo-Pacific, with the main population located near Hawaii and Sri Lanka. It prefers to stay hidden in coral reef, rock formation, or in shade. They are mainly active during the daytime, congregating near sea sponge to feed.
