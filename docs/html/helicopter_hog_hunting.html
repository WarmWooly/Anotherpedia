Helicopter hog hunting

Helicopter hog hunting or pork choppering is a method of wild hog hunting within the United States where participants fire upon wild boars from a flown helicopter. As wild boars are an invasive species within the United States, hunting regulation for them is much looser compared to other game. The primary goal of helicopter hog hunting is to reduce the population of the wild boars by slaying them, with the additional thrill generated by firing from a helicopter, which makes it easier to keep a safe distance away while keeping up with the target. Various services are offered to allow the guns and the helicopter to be rented for a scheduled hunt.
