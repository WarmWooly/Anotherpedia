Horizon

<><>The {{bhorizon}} is the line between the [[surface]] of a [[celestial body]] and the [[sky]] based on the [[perspective]] of an [[observer]]. The [[true horizon]] is the point where the [[view]] from the observer crosses the surface of the celestial body as if there were no [[obstructions|obstruction]] or [[refraction|atmospheric refraction]], while the [[apparent horizon]] takes the effects of the [[atmosphere]] into account. The [[visible horizon]] is the point where the view from the observer actually meets the sky, taking obstructions into account. The [[astronomical horizon]] is a [[right angle]] from the [[zenith]] of the observer extending outwards.
