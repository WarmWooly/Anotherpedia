Independent clause

An independent clause is a complete thought that has a noun and a verb. An independent clause can be used on its own, or conjoined with dependent or other independent clauses. A singular independent clause:The cat ate.An dependent clause following the independent clause:The cat ate because it was hungry.Two independent clauses connected with a comma (,):The cat ate, and the dog drank.Two independent clauses connected with a semicolon (;):The dog drank all the water; the cat got thirsty.
