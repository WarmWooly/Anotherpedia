Kieserite

Kieserite (magnesium sulphate monohydrate; MgSO4•H2O) is a hydrous magnesium sulfate. It can be colorless, white, or yellowish, while appearing clear within light. It has a molecular weight of 138.38 g/mol, with its name in honor of German doctor Dietrich Georg von Kieser. Kiserite can be found within marine salt deposits alongside anhydrite, halite, and polyhalite, which is most found in deposits around Germany and Russia. It is sold as a fertilizer to increase the magnisum content in soil to ensure crops do not undergo magnesium deficiency.
