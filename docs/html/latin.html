Latin

Latin is a language used by the Roman empire that originated in the Italy region of Latium. Latin had two distinct types: Classical Latin, used for formal document and poetry, and Vulgar Latin, spoken by the masses. Vulgar Latin is the key component to many romance language, such as French, Portuguese, and Romanian.&pThe alphabet of Latin had the following 23 letter (linguistics): A B C D E F G H I K L M N O P Q R S T V X Y Z&pMany phrases, such as et cetera, ad hoc, and quid pro quo came from Latin.
