Latin

Latin is a language used by the Roman empire that originated in the Italian region of Latium. Latin had two distinct types: Classical Latin, used for formal documents and poetry, and Vulgar Latin, spoken by the masses. Vulgar Latin is the key component to many romance languages, such as French, Portuguese, and Romanian.&pThe alphabet of Latin had the following 23 letters: A B C D E F G H I K L M N O P Q R S T V X Y Z&pMany phrases, such as et cetera, ad hoc, and quid pro quo came from Latin.
