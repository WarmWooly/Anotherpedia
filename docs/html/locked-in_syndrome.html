Locked-in Syndrome

<><><>{{bLocked-in Syndrome}} ({{bLiS}}) is a [[disorder]] to the [[nervous system]] who are [[paralyzed|paralysis]] but are still able to control their [[eyelids|eyelid]] and are still [[conscious]] (despite being unable to [[speak]]). This rare disorder may arise from a [[traumatic brain injury]], [[overdosing|overdose]] on [[medication]], a [[disease]] in the [[circulatory system]], or a disease that damages and destroys the [[myelin sheath]] of [[nerve cells|nerve cell]], which in turn affect the [[pons]] on the [[brainstem]]. Most [[people]] with LiS can still [[communicate|communication]] through moving their [[eyes|eye]] or eyelids. LiS is found to be most common in people between 30 and 50.
