Locked-in Syndrome

<>Locked-in Syndrome (LiS) is a disorder to the nervous system who are paralysis but are still able to control their eyelid and are still conscious (despite being unable to speak). This rare disorder may arise from a traumatic brain injury, overdose on medication, a disease in the circulatory system, or a disease that damages and destroys the myelin sheath of nerve cell, which in turn affect the pons on the brainstem. Most people with LiS can still communication through moving their eye or eyelids. LiS is found to be most common in people between 30 and 50.
