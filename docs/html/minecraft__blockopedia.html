Minecraft: Blockopedia

{{iMinecraft: Blockopedia}} is a 2015 book written by Alex Wiltshire under Mojang and published by Scholastic, acting as an encyclopedia for blocks within Minecraft, specifically Minecraft: Java Edition 1.8. It is a hardcover book made in the shape of a hexagon, matching the shape of a block as displayed as an item. The book is 312 pages long. In 2021, an updated edition would be released. The book is uniquely hexagonal, with its hardcover being textured on the dark parts of the grass block and the pebbles within. The title is slightly reflective and silver in color, matching the angles of the block's faces. The book has a black ribbon connected within to act as a bookmark. The book came in a black, rectangular cardboard case with the title along the side in reflective silver and a plastic opening in the shape of the book.

Each block will have a full image of itself on the left side, with block information and description to the right. Some blocks have additional pages of content to talk about additional feature, crafting recipes, or similar blocks (like diorite, andesite, and granite under stone). In the background of the information section of the block, an example image will be shown of the block used in a build or its appearance in natural generation. Shaders are used throughout the book for background photos, creating realistic shadows and making some blocks reflective. When other blocks are brought up in the book, page numbers will be included for the block when applicable.
