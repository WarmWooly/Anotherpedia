mRPG

mRPG was a text messaging-based virtual tabletop (VTT) mobile app for playing tabletop role-playing game such as Dungeons & Dragons that allowed for users to send texts, image, dice simulation, macro (computer science), and character sheet. mRPG was started in 2018 by Adriano Cola and shut down at the end of 2024. Felipe Fabel was also noted as one of the "Creators of mRPG" in their shutting down message on the mRPG app. In 2022, the development of mRPG 2Ed (second edition) was announced, overhauling the app's design and features with the promise of cross compatability for desktop app, finishing and launching the mobile app changes the following year. The desktop app was never released to the public.

Various features of mRPG made it notable and useful as a lightweight VTT, mainly dice rolling and a character management system. Rolling dice was done through a custom-built function system that allowed for any number and size of dice to be rolled, to run function (computer science) on the results, and to display text and emoji outcomes based on programmed criteria. Campaigns could be openly advertised to all users of the app, having filters for the system used, language spoken, the content maturity of the content, and various other aspects. The character system allowed players to chat as their characters and keep track of their stats and inventory (mechanic) on customizable and shareable character sheets. The game master would also have access to chat as a "GM" or as a spontaneously created Non-player character.
