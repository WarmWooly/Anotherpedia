mRPG

mRPG was a texting-based virtual tabletop (VTT) mobile app for playing tabletop role-playing games such as Dungeons & Dragons that allowed for users to send texts, images, dice rolls, macros, and character sheets. mRPG was started in 2018 by Adriano Cola and shut down at the end of 2024.<> Felipe Fabel was also noted as one of the "Creators of mRPG" in their shutting down message on the mRPG app. In 2022, the development of mRPG 2Ed (second edition) was announced,<> overhauling the app's design and features with the promise of cross compatability for desktop, finishing and launching the mobile app changes the following year. The desktop app was never released to the public.&pVarious features of mRPG made it notable and useful as a lightweight VTT, mainly dice rolling and a character management system. Rolling dice was done through a custom-built function system that allowed for any number and size of dice to be rolled, to run functions on the results, and to display text and emoji outcomes based on programmed criteria. Campaigns could be openly advertised to all users of the app, having filters for the system used, language spoken, the maturity of the content, and various other aspects. The character system allowed players to chat as their characters and keep track of their stats and inventory on customizable and shareable character sheets. The game master would also have access to chat as a "GM" or as a spontaneously created NPCs.
