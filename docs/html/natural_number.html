Natural number

<><>A {{bnatural number}} is a [[whole number]] which is [[positive|positive number]]. It comes from how [[natural]] [[counting]] consists of [[whole]] elements, such as a [[log]] or an [[apple]]. It does not consider the [[fraction]] of an [[object]], [[quantifying]] elements into the simplest [[numeric]] form. Natural numbers may or may not include [[0]], as there isn't a [[physical]] way to [[represent|representation]] [[nothing]], but it is a whole number. Natural numbers include [[1]], [[2]], [[3]], [[4]], [[5]], and so on. The [[set|set (mathematics)]] of all natural numbers is denoted with [[bold|bold (typography)]] {{bN}} or [[blackboard bold]] &bbN. The set of natural numbers is a subset of [[integers|integer]] &bbZ.
