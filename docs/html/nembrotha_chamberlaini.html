Nembrotha chamberlaini

{{bNembrotha chamberlaini}} is a species of sea slug, being a nudibranch (a gastropod Mollusca) in the family (biology) of Polyceridae. It was first described in 1997 by Terrence M. Gosliner and David W. Behrens. Growing to nearly 4 inch long, a Nembrotha chamberlaini is very colorful, with a base of white along its underside and a dark maroon/back on its top. Down its underbelly is a stripe of purple with a yellow coloration along the sides. A lighter red colors the chamberlaini's behind, branchial plume, and rhinophores, with additional yellow surrounding the plume. It also has a pair of blue/purple oral tentacles. It can be found around Indonesia and the Philippines feeding on sea squirt.
