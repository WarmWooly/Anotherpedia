Occupational Safety and Health Administration

The Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) is a regulatory agency under the United States Department of Labor lead by the Assistant Secretary of Labor for Occupational Safety and Health Douglas L. Parker <>. OSHA was created after President Richard Nixon signed the Occupational Safety and Health Act by the United States Congress on December 29, 1970. The purpose of OSHA is to increase worker safety by regulating standards in order to avoid the risk of injury, disfigurement, or death. OSHA also protects whistleblowers from retaliation when revealing safety hazards.
