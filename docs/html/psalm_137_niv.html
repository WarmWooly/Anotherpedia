Psalm 137 NIV

In the New International Version of the 5th book of Psalms, the 137th chapter reads:s-p1By the rivers of Babylon we sat and wept
when we remembered Zion.
s-p2There on the poplars
we hung our harps,
s-p3for there our captors asked us for songs,
our tormentors demanded songs of joy;
they said, “Sing us one of the songs of Zion!”

s-p4How can we sing the songs of the Lord
while in a foreign land?
s-p5If I forget you, Jerusalem,
may my right hand forget its skill.
s-p6May my tongue cling to the roof of my mouth
if I do not remember you,
if I do not consider Jerusalem
my highest joy.

s-p7Remember, Lord, what the Edomites did
on the day Jerusalem fell.
“Tear it down,” they cried,
“tear it down to its foundations!”
s-p8Daughter Babylon, doomed to destruction,
happy is the one who repays you
according to what you have done to us.
s-p9Happy is the one who seizes your infants
and dashes them against the rocks.This is following Psalm 136 NIV and before Psalm 138 NIV.
