Psalm 137 NIV

In the New International Version of the Psalms (Book 5) of Psalms, the 137th chapter (Psalms) reads:s-pPsalm 137:1 NIVBy the rivers of Babylon we sat and wept
when we remembered Zion.
s-pPsalm 137:2 NIVThere on the poplars
we hung our harps,
s-pPsalm 137:3 NIVfor there our captors asked us for songs,
our tormentors demanded songs of joy;
they said, “Sing us one of the songs of Zion!”

s-pPsalm 137:4 NIVHow can we sing the songs of the Lord
while in a foreign land?
s-pPsalm 137:5 NIVIf I forget you, Jerusalem,
may my right hand forget its skill.
s-pPsalm 137:6 NIVMay my tongue cling to the roof of my mouth
if I do not remember you,
if I do not consider Jerusalem
my highest joy.

s-pPsalm 137:7 NIVRemember, Lord, what the Edomites did
on the day Jerusalem fell.
“Tear it down,” they cried,
“tear it down to its foundations!”
s-pPsalm 137:8 NIVDaughter Babylon, doomed to destruction,
happy is the one who repays you
according to what you have done to us.
s-pPsalm 137:9 NIVHappy is the one who seizes your infants
and dashes them against the rocks.This is following Psalm 136 NIV and before Psalm 138 NIV.
