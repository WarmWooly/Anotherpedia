Pseudoscience

<><>{{bPseudoscience}} is a [[collection]] of [[concepts|concept]], [[beliefs|belief]], and [[claims|claim]] that, despite appearing [[scientific]], are not consistent with the [[scientific method]]. Pseudoscience often relies on [[misunderstandings|misunderstanding]] and [[falsehoods|falsehood]] to seem [[legitimate|legitimacy]], even when [[contradicting|contradiction]] established [[theories|scientific theory]], [[observations|observation]], or [[reality]] itself. While pseudoscience is generally considered to be [[negative]], the effects on [[society]] can vary from being a simple [[misconception]] to causing [[bodily harm]] and [[death]].&pPseudoscience may be used for [[monetary|monetary incentive]] or [[political incentives|political incentive]], reaffirming an [[worldview]], or to position oneself as an [[authority figure]] in a [[field|scientific field]]. Pseudoscience is closely related to [[conspiracy theories|conspiracy theory]] and [[science denial]], utilizing [[anecdotes|anecote]] of how it applies with [[cherry-picked|cherry picking]] examples to support it while suppressing contradictory [[evidence]]. The amount of pushback on a pseudoscientific idea may be used to the spreader's advantage, weaponizing the idea of going against the "[[mainstream narrative]]" and "[[the establishment]]".&pPseudoscience comes in many forms and sizes, from [[local traditions|local tradition]] that only affect small [[communities|community]] to affecting entire [[countries|country]] or the [[world|humanity]]. The field of pseudoscience can also vary, with examples including [[perpetual motion]] in [[physical science]], [[autistic enterocolitis]] in [[medical science]], and [[Holocaust denial]] in [[social science]] and [[history]].
