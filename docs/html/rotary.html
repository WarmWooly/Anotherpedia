Rotary

A rotary is a round intersection (road) with an Island (road) in the center. There is no requirement to slow down when entering a rotary. Most rotaries have four entrances and exits.

In the United States, drivers enter and go counterclockwise around the rotary. If there is one lane entering, a driver can stay right or Merge (driving) into the rotary. If there are two lanes entering, the right lane usually sticks to going right while the left lane must merge.

Rotaries are much less safe compared to Roundabout due to the higher speeds and the requirement to merge to get off the majority of exits. They are made to increase the flow of traffic and eliminate full stops on the road.
