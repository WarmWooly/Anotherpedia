Roundabout

A roundabout is a round intersection (road) with an Island (road) in the center. Every car entering a roundabout must Yield (driving) to traffic inside the roundabout. Most roundabouts have four entrances and exits.

In the United States, drivers enter and go counterclockwise around the roundabout. Some roundabouts have two lanes, where the left lane is used for left and U-Turn, while the right lane is used for right turns and going straight.

Roundabouts remove the possibility of a t-bone and decrease the severity of any Car Crash that happen inside. They are also made to increase the flow of traffic and helps reduce full stops on the road.

Rotary and Traffic Circle are both used as Synonym to a roundabout, but they are also their own unique intersection type.
