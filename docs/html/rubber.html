Rubber

For other terms relating to rubber, see rubber (disambiguation).
Rubber is an elastic material that can be made naturally (using latex sap) or synthetically (using elastomers) that is used in a plethora of objects, such as tires, gloves, balloons, rubber bands, erasers, sports balls, and more. The process to harden rubber is known as vulcanization. Lots of rubber is black, but they can be easily colored.
