Salt pan (geology)

<><>A {{bsalt pan}}, {{bsalt flat}}, or {{bsalt plain}}, is a [[natural]] [[geographic feature]] consting of [[salt]] and other [[minerals|mineral]] resulting from a [[body of water]] (such as [[lakes|lake]]) [[evaporating|evaporation]] in a [[desert]]. The salt and minerals pile up over time, covering the surface. Nearly all salt pans are white, and many have a [[cell]]-like pattern on their surface.
