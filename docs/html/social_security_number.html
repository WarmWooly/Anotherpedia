Social Security number

<><>A {{bSocial Security number}} is an [[id|Identification]] that consists of nine numeric characters in the format 000-00-0000. It is required in the [[US|United States]] to apply for [[jobs|Job]], making [[bank]] transactions, filling out [[taxes|Tax]], applying for a government assistance program, obtaining a [[driver's licence]], [[renting|Rent]]/purchasing [[housing|House]], applying for [[insurance]], and other institutional purposes.<>The first three numbers are the Area Number, based on the [[ZIP|Zone Improvement Plan]] of the [[mailing address]] for the application. The next two numbers are the Group Number, which used to be based on a specific pattern. The last four numbers are the Serial Number, which is assigned from 0001-9999.<>The modern method to assign numbers is pure [[randomization|Random]]. This greatly decreases the chance of identity theft because, compared to the old system, a single-digit change is less likely to be a correct SSN.
