Stripping (Minecraft)

For the action of removing wax from a block, see scraping ({{aiMinecraft).}}&spStripping is an action in Minecraft of converting log-type blocks into a version with the top layer (mostly bark) "removed". Once a block is stripped, it cannot be unstripped. To strip a block, use any axe on the wood. Stripping wood takes 1 durability from the axe per use (not accounting for Unbreaking). There is no delay between stripping blocks. Logs and wood are used as decorative blocks to make more "realistic"-looking bases and may be used as a part of the design. Since it only takes one misinput to strip a block, there are many stories where players report that they accidentally stripped a part of their base, with many memes cropping from such a phenomenon, especially on Reddit. A popular variation of the meme is when after a player accidentally strips one log, they go around their entire base and strip the rest to match.
