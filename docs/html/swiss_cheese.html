Swiss cheese

For cheeses from Switzerland, see Swiss cheeses.
Swiss cheese is a popular medium-hard cheese that is light-yellow in color, well-known for the holes, or "eyes," inside the cheese. Swiss cheese originates from Emmental, Switzerland, but Swiss cheese is a general term that does not specify that the cheese must be from Switzerland. Swiss cheese is now produced in the United States, Finland, Ireland, and Estonia alongside Switzerland. "Swiss cheese," "Swiss-style cheese," and "Emmental cheese" may be used interchangeably in some contexts. Values listed for 100 g of standard Swiss cheese. % based on the Daily Value of 100 g.
