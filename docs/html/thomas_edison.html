Thomas Edison

{{bThomas Alva Edison}} was an Americans inventor who is well-known for his invention of the phonograph and the incandescent light bulb. He was born on February 11, 1847 in Ohio, but then lived his childhood in Michigan.&pAs a teenager, he newspaper hawker newspaper, candy, and vegetable to people going on train, saving up for equipment for his experiment. He even created his own paper, {{iGrand Trunk Herald}}, which he sold with the rest of his papers. He later moved to Louisville, Kentucky, Kentucky to work for Western Union when he was 19, where in 1867 he was firing for spilling sulfiric acid from a lead-acid battery, which went through the floor onto the desk of his boss.&pOn June 1, 1869, Thomas Edison made his first patent for an electric vote recorder (U.S. Patent 90646).&pOn October 18, 1931, Edison death at the age of 84.
