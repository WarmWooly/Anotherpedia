Traffic circle

A {{btraffic circle}} is a round intersection (road) with an Island (road) in the center. Depending on the location, there may be a stop sign, a yield sign, or even no sign at all. Most traffic circles have four entrances and exits.&pIn the United States, drivers enter and go counterclockwise around the traffic circle. The outside lane is allowed to exit at any point, while the inside lane must Merge (driving) to the outside lane before exiting.&pTraffic circles are much less safe than Roundabout due to the ability to get T-Bone and how many traffic circles require you to quickly speed onto it. They are usually made for appeal and not for efficiency.
