Vietnam War

The Vietnam War was an armed conflict in Vietnam and surrounding countries from November 1, 1955 to 30 April 30, 1975, spanning nearly 19.5 years. The conflict was between North Vietnam and it's organizations Viet Cong and the Provisional Revolutionary Government of the Republic of South Vietnam (PRG), the Lao People's Liberation Army (from Laos), Khmer Rouge (from Cambodia), Royal Government of the National Union of Kampuchea (GRUNK, also from Cambodia), China, the Soviet Union, and North Korea against South Vietnam, the United States, South Korea, Australia, New Zeland, Laos, Cambodia and it's political party Khmer Republic, Thailand, and the Philippines. The United States lost the war, turning North and South Vietnam into a single country as Vietnam.

In total, it is estimated that there were between 1.3 million and 3.4 million casulties. There were multiple war crimes committed by both sides of the war, including terrorism, bombings of civilian targets, widespread torture, and more.
