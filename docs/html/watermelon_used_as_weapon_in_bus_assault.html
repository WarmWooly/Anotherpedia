Watermelon used as weapon in bus assault

The news story {{iWatermelon used as weapon in bus assault}} was write by Ruby Jones and publish by the Australian Broadcasting Corporation on January 22, 2013. The <> is quite open and vague due to how recent the event was. It describes how the Northern Territory (Australia) police arrest a person who was 40 years old for smashing a watermelon on the top of the bus driver head in the northern suburbs of Darwin, northern territory. Police reported that the conflict started when the driver asked for the fare. Debbie Gabolinscy, who was watching the event unfold, claims that the man exited the bus and then re-entered to continue assault the driver. Passenger nearby separated the driver and assailant, with the driver sustaining minor injuries. The man was arrested afterwards. There is a blog titled <> on the website Blogger under Nothing to Do With Arbroath by the user arbroath (Blogger) on January 24, 2013, 6:06 AM that discussed the event. It describes how it was a bus owned by Buslink, and identifies the location of the incident at the Hibiscus Shopping Centre in Leanyer, Northern Territory (a suburb in Darwin) at around noon. The driver asked the drunk man to exit the bus, to where Jones made the driver flinch until smashing the watermelon, to then throw a punch, which broke the driver's glasses. Allegedly, his four child were watching the assault unfold. On VICE, a story written by Toby Fehily titled <> on January 24, 2013, 6:37 PM was posted to the site. In relation to the watermelon story, it re-states the bus's location and actions, omitting the punch from arborath's blog. The assailant was identified as Raymond Jones, a 53-year-old man who drank roughy 700 mililiter of burbon from Wild Turkey before the event. He later pled guilty in court for the charges of aggravated assault and disorderly behavior. Due to the absurdity of the situation, people created internet meme about the story in 2021 and onwards. The main video, created by Meh_here (YouTube), titled watermelon used as weapon in bus assault meme- pvz meme, is a video that lasts 27 second with over 3,500 view (YouTube) <>. The content of the video is the bus image from the original article with an overlay of the Melon-pult (Plants vs. Zombies) from Plants vs. Zombies, making fun of the scenario by hinting at the idea that Jones was acting like the plant.
