Wisteria

Wisteria is a genus of a flowering plant legume that is known for its vibrant color and vine-like nature. It was classified in 1818 by Thomas Nuttall, which was specifically the American wisteria.

Wisteria grows by twisting its stem around tall structures, such as tree. It has leaf that grow along the vine, alongside flower and pod, which contain Wisteria's seed. The plant contains wisterin, a saponin of Wisteria, which is poisonous. The seeds are also highly poisonous on digestion.

Wisteria is found in many parts of the world, such as China, Vietnam, Korea, Japan, northern Iran, eastern United States, and southern Canada. There are currently four species of Wisteria that are accepted (in alphabetical order):
Silky wisteria
Japanese wisteria
American wisteria
Chinese wisteria
