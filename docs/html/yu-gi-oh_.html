{{iYu-Gi-Oh!}}

<><>{{b{{iYu-Gi-Oh!}}}} is a [[franchise]] made by [[Kazuki Takahashi]] in 1996. The original [[Japanese]] name for {{iYu-Gi-Oh!}}, {{i遊☆戯☆王}}, is translated to [[English]] as "King of Games". {{iYu-Gi-Oh!}} started off as a [[manga]] series. Before the [[card game]], most people knew {{iYu-Gi-Oh!}} for the manga where [[Yugi Mutou|Yugi Mutou (Yu-Gi-Oh!)]] and other cast members played all sorts of games. Eventually, Kazuki Takahashi made what would later become the card game. It was originally known in-series as {{iMagic & Wizards}}, and later on became known as {{iDuel Monsters}}. The readers of the series loved the card game so much to the point where Kazuki Takahashi made it the main focus of the story and it eventually became the card game of the series.<>The real-world card game of {{iYu-Gi-Oh!}} is separated into two different versions. These versions are called the {{iYu-Gi-Oh! Original Card Game}}, and the {{iYu-Gi-Oh! Trading Card Game}}. The {{iYu-Gi-Oh! Original Card Game}} is commonly [[abbreviated|abbreviation]] as "OCG", and similarly the {{iYu-Gi-Oh! Trading Card Game}} is abbreviated as "TCG".<>The OCG is the version of the game played primarily in [[Japan]], [[South Korea]], [[Hong Kong]], [[Indonesia]], [[Malaysia]], [[Philippines]], [[Singapore]], [[Taiwan]], and [[Thailand]]. The OCG has an entirely separate [[banlist|Banlist (Yu-Gi-Oh!)]] from the TCG. Cards from the TCG are not legal for use in the OCG due to a difference in the card backs.&sp<>The TCG is the version of the game played primarily worldwide, but mainly in [[America]], [[Europe]], [[Latin America]], and [[Australia]]. The TCG cards are produced by the [[Belgian|Belgium]] [[company]] of [[Cartamundi]].
