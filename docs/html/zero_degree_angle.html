Zero degree angle

<><>A {{bzero degree angle}}, also known as a {{bzero angle}}, as implied in its name, is an [[angle]] of 0 [[degrees|degree (angle)]], or 0 [[radians|radian]]. Zero degree angles share all the same [[points|point (geometry)]] because they overlap each other. Zero degree angles may be mistaken as a single [[ray|ray (geometry)]] as they share the same angle. They are often shown at different lengths so both rays are visible (such as O-->A-->B) to avoid this.
